Hello! My name is Kangning Diao (刁康宁), and I am currently a postdoc at BCCP, UC Berkeley working with Prof. Uros Seljak. I obtained my Ph.D. under the supervision of [Prof. Yi Mao](http://i.astro.tsinghua.edu.cn/~ymao/) at Tsinghua University, with a focus on statistical methods in 21 cm cosmology. My research spanning from 21 cm signal, Galactic synchrotron to weak lensing and exoplanets, with a particular emphasis on employing statistical methods. In terms of programming, I am proficient in Python and frequently utilize packages such as `JAX` and `PyTorch`.

I am a cautious person, and I believe my personality drives my research taste. I am particularly interested in statistical validity. Under this broad scope, my work spans from classical methods to AI applications. For the former, I am working on the statistical validation of exoplanet transit light curves using persistent sampling. For the latter, I developed a [Microcanonical Langevin Monte Carlo](https://arxiv.org/pdf/2212.08549)-based training method, [SMILE](https://arxiv.org/abs/2602.06500), to train Bayesian neural networks that are aware of their own uncertainties. Additionally, I recast the problem of finding new effects in observations as an out-of-distribution detection problem, and am working on detecting those with generative models such as Flow Matching. Preliminary results can be found in [this paper](https://ui.adsabs.harvard.edu/abs/2025JCAP...08..004D).
